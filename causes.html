🧬 Causes of Sickle Cell Disease

Sickle Cell Disease (SCD) is caused by **genetic mutations** in the hemoglobin gene. It is an inherited condition, meaning it is passed down from parents to children. Understanding the causes is essential for awareness and family planning.

Genetic Cause

SCD occurs when a person inherits **two abnormal hemoglobin genes (HbS)**, one from each parent. This is known as an **autosomal recessive inheritance pattern**.

  • Two HbS genes: The child will have Sickle Cell Disease.
  • One HbS gene + one normal gene: The child will have sickle cell trait and usually does not show symptoms, but can pass the gene to children.
  • One HbS gene + another abnormal hemoglobin gene (e.g., HbC or Beta-thalassemia): The severity of SCD varies depending on the combination of genes inherited.

Why it happens

The abnormal hemoglobin (HbS) changes the shape of red blood cells into a sickle or crescent form. These cells are rigid, sticky, and break down faster than normal red blood cells, causing anemia, pain, and other complications.

Genetic counseling is recommended for families with a history of SCD to understand inheritance risks and make informed decisions.
